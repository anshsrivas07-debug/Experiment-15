## 3. Concepts Used

### 3.1 Natural Language Processing (NLP)
Natural Language Processing is a branch of artificial intelligence that enables computers to understand, interpret, and process human language in both written and spoken form.

---

### 3.2 Tokenization
Tokenization is the process of breaking text into smaller units such as words or sentences, known as tokens.

---

### 3.3 Stop Words
Stop words are commonly used words (like "is", "the", "and") that do not carry significant meaning and are often removed to improve processing efficiency.

---

### 3.4 Stemming
Stemming reduces words to their root form by removing suffixes.

---

### 3.5 Lemmatization
Lemmatization converts words into their base or dictionary form using proper linguistic rules.

---

### 3.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging
POS tagging assigns grammatical labels such as noun, verb, adjective, etc., to each word.

---

### 3.7 Frequency Distribution

## 4. Theory

### 4.1 Introduction to Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is widely used in modern applications such as **chatbots, search engines, machine translation, speech recognition, and sentiment analysis**. Since human language is complex and unstructured, NLP techniques are used to convert it into structured data that computers can analyze efficiently.

---

### 4.2 Tokenization

Tokenization is the first step in text preprocessing. It divides text into smaller meaningful units.

#### Types of Tokenization:

- **Word Tokenization:** Splits text into individual words  
- **Sentence Tokenization:** Splits text into sentences  

#### Importance:
- Helps in breaking down complex text  
- Forms the foundation for further NLP operations  
- Makes analysis easier and more structured  

---

### 4.3 Stop Word Removal

Stop words are frequently used words that do not contribute significantly to the meaning of the text.

#### Examples:
is, the, in, on, at, and, of  

#### Importance:
- Reduces size of dataset  
- Improves processing speed  
- Focuses on meaningful and important words  

---

### 4.4 Stemming

Stemming is the process of reducing words to their root form by removing suffixes.

#### Examples:
- Playing → Play  
- Studies → Studi  

#### Characteristics:
- Simple and fast  
- May produce words that are not grammatically correct  

---

### 4.5 Lemmatization

Lemmatization converts words into their correct base form using vocabulary and grammar rules.

#### Examples:
- Running → Run  
- Better → Good  

#### Characteristics:
- Produces meaningful words  
- More accurate than stemming  
- Slightly slower but more reliable  

---

### 4.6 Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging

POS tagging identifies the grammatical role of each word in a sentence.

#### Common Tags:
- **NN:** Noun  
- **NNP:** Proper Noun  
- **VB:** Verb  
- **JJ:** Adjective  
- **DT:** Determiner  

#### Importance:
- Helps in understanding sentence structure  
- Useful in grammar checking and language modeling  
- Improves accuracy in NLP tasks  

---

### 4.7 Frequency Distribution

Frequency distribution calculates how often each word appears in a text.

#### Importance:
- Identifies frequently used words  
- Helps in keyword extraction  
- Useful for text summarization and analysis  

---

### 4.8 Importance of NLP Techniques

- Converts unstructured text into structured data  
- Enables machines to understand human language  
- Improves information retrieval and search systems  
- Used in chatbots, translators, recommendation systems  
- Helps in sentiment analysis and document classification  
- Plays a key role in artificial intelligence and data science
